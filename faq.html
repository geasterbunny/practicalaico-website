COMMON AI QUESTIONS
What is the best AI tool for office workers?
For most office workers, ChatGPT is the best starting point — it handles emails, summaries, brainstorming, and research well. Claude is excellent for longer documents and nuanced writing. Microsoft Copilot is ideal if your workplace uses Microsoft 365. Gemini integrates well with Google Workspace. The best tool depends on what software you already use at work. Our free Beginners Guide covers all four and helps you choose.
Is ChatGPT safe to use at work?
ChatGPT is generally safe for non-confidential tasks like drafting emails, brainstorming ideas, and summarising publicly available information. However, you should never paste client names, financial data, passwords, HR information, or anything marked confidential into the free version. Paid plans like ChatGPT Plus have stronger data policies. Always check whether your employer has an AI usage policy before using any tool. Read our full guide: AI at Work — What's Safe to Type.
Do I need to know coding to use AI at work?
No. Modern AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are designed for plain English conversations. You type what you need in normal language and the AI responds. No programming, no technical setup, no command line. Every guide at The Practical AI Co. is built for non-technical professionals.
How do I write better prompts for AI?
Good prompts have four parts: WHO the output is for, WHAT you need, WHY (the context), and any CONSTRAINTS like tone, length, or format. For example, instead of "write an email," try "write a professional email to my manager requesting annual leave next Friday, keep it under 100 words, friendly tone." The more specific you are, the better the output. See our full guide: The One Thing That Matters About AI at Work.
Can AI take meeting notes automatically?
Yes. Tools like Otter.ai, Fireflies, and Microsoft Copilot can join your Zoom or Teams calls, transcribe the conversation, and generate summaries with action items automatically. If recording isn't allowed, you can paste rough notes into ChatGPT or Claude after the meeting and ask it to create a structured summary with decisions, action items, and follow-ups. Full walkthrough: Meeting Notes in 90 Seconds.
What is the difference between ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini?
ChatGPT (by OpenAI) is the most widely used and best for general tasks. Claude (by Anthropic) excels at long documents, careful analysis, and nuanced writing. Gemini (by Google) integrates directly with Google Workspace and is strong for research and search-related tasks. All three are free to start and handle common office tasks well. Our Start Here: Chatbots guide has ready-to-use workflows for all three.
Will AI replace my job?
AI is more likely to change your job than replace it entirely. Roles that involve repetitive writing, data entry, scheduling, and basic research are being augmented by AI tools. Professionals who learn to use AI effectively become more productive and more valuable. The risk is greater for those who ignore AI than for those who adopt it.
How much does it cost to use AI tools at work?
ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini all offer free tiers that are sufficient for most daily tasks. Paid plans typically cost A$30–40 per month and offer faster responses, stronger privacy, and access to newer features. Microsoft Copilot is included in some Microsoft 365 business plans or available as an add-on.
What AI tools work with Microsoft Office?
Microsoft Copilot is built directly into Microsoft 365 and works inside Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams. It can draft documents, summarise email threads, create presentations from prompts, and analyse spreadsheet data. If you don't have Copilot, you can use ChatGPT or Claude alongside Office by copying content between them.
How do I convince my boss to let me use AI at work?
Start by using AI for low-risk tasks on your own time — drafting emails, preparing meeting summaries, or researching topics. Document the time you save. Then present specific examples to your manager showing before-and-after results. Emphasise that you're using it as a productivity tool, not a replacement for judgment, and that you're following safe data practices.
